# Product Owner Workflows

## Product Discovery

1. Capture business problem, customer problem, and opportunity.
2. Identify users, decision makers, and affected workflows.
3. Define product outcomes and success metrics.
4. Validate clinical, insurance, compliance, and technical constraints.
5. Decide MVP fit and discovery gaps.

## Requirement Lifecycle

1. Receive validated business requirement.
2. Confirm source, owner, and traceability.
3. Convert to product requirement, feature, and user stories.
4. Add acceptance criteria, risks, assumptions, and dependencies.
5. Move to backlog when Definition of Ready is met.

## Backlog Refinement

1. Review priority and MVP alignment.
2. Split oversized features.
3. Remove duplicate or conflicting stories.
4. Confirm QA readiness.
5. Sequence dependencies.

## Prioritization

1. Select prioritization framework.
2. Score value, risk, urgency, effort, dependencies, and compliance impact.
3. Separate Must Have from deferred scope.
4. Document rationale and tradeoffs.
5. Confirm with stakeholders.

## Release Planning

1. Define release goal and success metrics.
2. Confirm Must Have scope.
3. Sequence dependencies and validation gates.
4. Identify rollback or disablement path.
5. Prepare release recommendation.

## Sprint Planning

1. Select stories that meet Definition of Ready.
2. Confirm sprint goal.
3. Validate capacity, dependencies, and QA coverage.
4. Assign specialist handoffs.
5. Track risks and open questions.

## Change Management

1. Capture change request and reason.
2. Assess scope, risk, compliance, and release impact.
3. Decide approve, defer, split, or reject.
4. Update roadmap, backlog, and stakeholders.
5. Log decision and rationale.

## Risk Management

1. Identify product, clinical, insurance, compliance, technical, and operational risks.
2. Rate impact and probability.
3. Assign owner and mitigation.
4. Add acceptance criteria or validation tasks.
5. Review risk before release.

## Healthcare Product Workflow

1. Identify clinical workflow and user role.
2. Confirm patient safety and human review needs.
3. Define clinical documentation, SOAP, ICD, or evidence impact.
4. Route ambiguous clinical content to Clinical AI.
5. Add audit, privacy, and PDPA requirements.

## Insurance Product Workflow

1. Identify claim lifecycle stage and reviewer role.
2. Confirm payer policy, coverage, benefit, and waiting period assumptions.
3. Define claim readiness and evidence package expectations.
4. Route policy ambiguity to Insurance AI.
5. Preserve human claim decision authority.

## AI Feature Workflow

1. Define AI task and user decision point.
2. Confirm AI is advisory only.
3. Require confidence, explanation, and source traceability.
4. Define human accept, reject, override, and fallback states.
5. Add audit and quality validation scenarios.
