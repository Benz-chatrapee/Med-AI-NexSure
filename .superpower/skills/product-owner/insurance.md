# Product Owner Insurance Knowledge

## Claim Lifecycle

Claim workflows include documentation collection, evidence review, policy validation, claim readiness assessment, reviewer decision, submission, and post-submission follow-up.

## Coverage Rules

Coverage rules define whether a service may be eligible under a policy. Product features must show source and uncertainty when rules are incomplete.

## Benefit Rules

Benefit rules define limits, covered services, exclusions, and entitlement constraints. Human reviewers must validate benefit interpretation.

## Waiting Period

Waiting period checks require policy dates, coverage terms, service dates, and reviewer confirmation.

## Medical Necessity

Medical necessity must be supported by documentation and reviewer validation. AI may flag possible gaps but must not make final determinations.

## Policy Validation

Policy validation compares available documentation against known policy rules. Unknown or missing policy sources must be marked uncertain.

## Claim Readiness

Claim readiness summarizes whether documentation appears complete enough for human review. It must not approve or deny claims.

## Fraud Indicators

Product features may flag patterns for review, but fraud conclusions require authorized investigation and compliance handling.

## Audit Trail

Insurance workflows require traceability for AI output, reviewer decisions, overrides, evidence changes, and policy validation status.

## Compliance

Insurance product requirements must avoid fabricated policies, unsupported evidence, hidden uncertainty, and automatic claim decisions.

## Economic Intelligence

Economic intelligence may track operational cost, rework, cycle time, readiness quality, and denial trends without exposing protected data.

## Insurance KPIs

- Claim readiness rate
- Missing evidence rate
- Reviewer cycle time
- Override rate
- Policy uncertainty rate
- Evidence package completion rate
- Preventable rework rate
