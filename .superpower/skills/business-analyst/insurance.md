# Business Analyst Insurance Knowledge

## Claim Readiness Workflow

Claim readiness requirements structure documentation completeness, missing evidence, readiness status, explanation, confidence, reviewer state, and audit logging before submission.

## Evidence Package Requirements

Evidence package requirements should define required documents, source references, status, reviewer notes, export permissions, and audit events.

## Required Document Checklist

Common requirement categories include visit note, SOAP note, diagnosis evidence, prescription or procedure evidence, medical certificate, payer-specific documents, consent or authorization, and reviewer decision record.

## Insurance Rule Validation

Insurance rule validation requirements must identify rule source, condition, expected evidence, exception path, uncertainty state, and reviewer authority.

## Missing Evidence Detection

Missing evidence detection should show what is missing, why it matters, source context, confidence, and recommended reviewer action.

## Claim Risk Levels

- Low: Evidence appears complete and policy source is available.
- Medium: Evidence gaps or policy ambiguity exists.
- High: Critical documentation missing, policy unavailable, or compliance risk exists.

## Coverage Indicator

Coverage indicators must be framed as decision support and should include source policy, confidence, uncertainty, and human reviewer confirmation.

## Payer Rule Setting

Payer rule requirements must include policy source, effective date, version, owner, validation state, exception handling, and audit trail.

## Audit and Compliance Requirements

Claim workflows must log readiness generation, evidence changes, reviewer overrides, policy validation state, and final human decision where applicable.

## OIC and PDPA Awareness

Requirements must recognize insurance governance, privacy, data minimization, access control, auditability, and policy interpretation boundaries. Route legal or regulatory interpretation to Compliance.

## Explainability Requirement

Claim-related AI outputs must include explanation, evidence references, confidence, uncertainty state, and human review requirement.

## Claim Decision Boundary

The Business Analyst must never approve or reject a claim as final authority. It may only structure requirements for decision support, review routing, and evidence readiness.
